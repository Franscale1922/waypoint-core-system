# Franchise Readiness Checklist

**Kelsey Stuart | Waypoint Franchise Advisors | waypointfranchise.com**

---

This is the checklist I use before any first conversation. It is not about a specific industry or brand. It is about whether you are actually in a position to start this process seriously.

Go through each question honestly. The score at the end will tell you where you stand.

---

## Section 1: Financial Position

- [ ] Do you have liquid capital available (cash, brokerage, or accessible retirement funds) to invest without putting your household in a precarious position?
- [ ] Have you mapped your monthly personal expenses and know the minimum income this business needs to cover them?
- [ ] Do you have reserves set aside to cover your personal cost of living for 12 months while a new business ramps up?
- [ ] Have you discussed your investment comfort level with your spouse or partner, if applicable, and are you both aligned?
- [ ] Do you understand the difference between your liquid capital and the total investment a franchise will require, including working capital and build-out?

---

## Section 2: Lifestyle and Goals

- [ ] Can you describe what you want your day-to-day life to look like in three years, specifically enough that a business model could be designed around it?
- [ ] Do you know whether you want to be operationally involved in the business or managing it at a distance?
- [ ] Have you thought about whether your goal is to build and sell the business or build and keep it long term?
- [ ] Are you making this decision because you genuinely want it, not because you are between jobs or someone persuaded you this was a good idea?
- [ ] Do you understand that franchise ownership is a business with real operational demands, not a passive income source?

---

## Section 3: Time and Process Readiness

- [ ] Are you in a position to spend three to five hours a week on this research for the next two to three months without it conflicting with other priorities?
- [ ] Are you not in a financial emergency that would pressure you into a fast decision?
- [ ] Are you open to evaluating franchise concepts you have never heard of, not just recognizable names?
- [ ] Are you willing to talk with five to ten existing franchise owners before making a final decision?
- [ ] Do you understand that the franchise disclosure document is a legal document and not a sales brochure?

---

## Section 4: Personal Fit

- [ ] Have you thought honestly about whether you enjoy leading and managing other people?
- [ ] Are you comfortable operating within a defined system rather than building your own from scratch?
- [ ] Have you talked about this with anyone close to you who will be affected by this decision?

---

## Scoring Guide

Count how many boxes you checked.

**16 to 18: You are ready to have a real conversation.** Your foundation is solid and the process makes sense to start now. Book a call and we can focus on finding the right fit rather than getting basics in place.

**11 to 15: You are close, with some gaps worth addressing.** Look at which sections you did not check. Financial gaps are fixable. Lifestyle clarity gaps are worth sitting with longer before moving forward.

**6 to 10: More work to do before committing.** That is useful to know now. Identify the items you could not check and treat them as your preparation list. This is exactly the kind of foundation you want in place before you start evaluating brands.

**Fewer than 6: Not ready yet.** That is not a judgment. It is information. Most of these gaps are workable, but doing this process before they are addressed leads to poor decisions. Come back when more of these are yes.
