# Food Franchise Readiness Checklist

**Kelsey Stuart | Waypoint Franchise Advisors | waypointfranchise.com**

---

This is the checklist I work through before a first conversation with anyone seriously looking at a food or restaurant franchise. It is not designed to encourage you. It is designed to help you figure out whether you are actually ready, and if not, what you need to work on before you move forward.

Most people who come to me with interest in food franchising are genuinely excited about the category. That excitement is fine, but it is not a qualification. Go through each question honestly. The score at the end will tell you something useful.

---

## Section 1: Financial Readiness

- [ ] Do you have liquid capital (cash or accessible funds, not including retirement accounts) of at least the franchise's required investment, plus 20% as a buffer?
- [ ] Have you budgeted a cash reserve to cover debt service and personal living expenses for a minimum of 12 months while the unit ramps up?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 7 (the section of the franchise disclosure document that details the total estimated investment required to open) and mapped every cost line, including build-out, equipment, initial inventory, grand opening marketing, and working capital?
- [ ] Do you understand that your all-in investment is almost always higher than the low end of the FDD range, and have you planned for the midpoint or above?
- [ ] If you are financing through an SBA loan or ROBS structure, have you spoken with a lender who specializes in franchise financing and confirmed you qualify?

---

## Section 2: Understanding the Margin Structure

- [ ] Do you understand that food cost and labor together typically consume 50 to 70 percent of revenue before you pay rent or royalty?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 19 (the section where franchisors may voluntarily disclose financial performance data for existing units) for this specific brand and calculated realistic revenue based on reported average unit volumes?
- [ ] Have you run a break-even analysis using the franchisor's average unit volume, realistic food cost and labor percentages for your market, and the royalty rate, not just the headline investment number?
- [ ] Are you clear on the royalty structure, including whether it is a percentage of gross revenue or net revenue, and what marketing fund contributions are on top of that?
- [ ] Have you confirmed what labor costs actually look like in your specific market, not a national average?

---

## Section 3: Operational Temperament

- [ ] Are you comfortable managing hourly workers, including hiring, firing, scheduling, and training people who may not stay long?
- [ ] Have you managed a team before, or do you have a clear plan for how you will develop that skill set before you open?
- [ ] Do you understand that food service has an industry-wide annual staff turnover rate around 75 percent, and are you prepared to treat recruiting as an ongoing operational function, not a one-time task?
- [ ] Are you comfortable with repetition, enforcing standards, and doing the same quality checks every shift?
- [ ] If something breaks down, a key employee calls out, or a health inspection is scheduled, can you step in and run the floor?

---

## Section 4: Time and Presence Expectations

- [ ] Are you prepared to be present in the operation for the first six to twelve months, including opening and closing shifts?
- [ ] Have you had an honest conversation with your family or partner about what the launch period will actually look like, including weekends, holidays, and evenings?
- [ ] If you are currently employed full time, do you have a realistic transition plan, and have you confirmed with the franchisor whether absentee or semi-absentee ownership is actually achievable for this brand?
- [ ] Do you understand the difference between a brand that is marketed as semi-absentee and one where that model is actually documented in the financial performance section of the franchise disclosure document and confirmed by existing franchisees?
- [ ] Are you physically comfortable with the environment, including long periods on your feet, kitchens, or food service settings?

---

## Section 5: Site Selection Awareness

- [ ] Do you understand that the franchisor typically drives or approves the site selection process, and that you will have limited control over which location you end up with?
- [ ] Have you identified the target market or territory you want to operate in, and confirmed that it is available?
- [ ] Do you know what the franchisor's site approval criteria are, and whether your preferred area has locations that would realistically meet those criteria?
- [ ] Has an independent commercial real estate advisor reviewed the lease terms for any site you are considering, separate from the franchisor's real estate team?
- [ ] Do you understand that a well-run food unit in a bad location rarely recovers, and have you pressure-tested the proposed site's traffic patterns, parking, and surrounding demographics independently?

---

## Section 6: Lifestyle Fit

- [ ] Have you spent time in the operating environment of this specific concept, either as a customer during different dayparts or, ideally, shadowing a current franchisee during operations?
- [ ] Has your answer to "why food?" been something other than "I love this brand" or "food is recession resistant"?
- [ ] Are you drawn to the actual work of running a food operation, meaning managing people, managing product, and managing a physical location, rather than just the idea of owning one?
- [ ] Have you thought through what this business looks like in year three, not just year one, and does that picture still fit the life you are trying to build?
- [ ] Are you prepared for a business that will likely require active involvement for at least five years before you have a truly self-managing operation?

---

## Section 7: Franchisor Due Diligence

- [ ] Have you read the full franchise disclosure document, not just the summary provided by the franchisor or their broker?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 19 (the section where franchisors may voluntarily disclose financial performance data for existing units) carefully, and do you understand what is and is not included in the numbers they are reporting?
- [ ] Have you spoken directly with at least five existing franchisees of this brand, people you contacted from the disclosure document's franchisee list rather than references handpicked by the franchisor?
- [ ] Have you asked those franchisees directly: "Would you buy this franchise again?"
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 20 (the section that shows how many franchisees have left the system, and how recently) to understand how many units have opened, transferred, or closed in the last three to five years, and does that trend concern you?
- [ ] Have you spoken with a franchise attorney who reviewed this specific franchise disclosure document before signing anything?

---

## Scoring Guide

Count how many boxes you checked.

**30 to 31: You are in a strong position.** You have done real work to understand what you are getting into. A first conversation with me is worth your time. We can focus on evaluating specific concepts rather than foundational preparation.

**22 to 29: Mostly ready, but there are gaps worth addressing.** Look at which sections you checked and which you did not. A few "no" answers in financial readiness or franchisor due diligence are fixable before moving forward. A pattern of "no" in operational temperament or lifestyle fit is worth sitting with longer.

**14 to 21: You have more work to do before committing.** That is not a reason to stop. It is information. Identify the categories where you scored lowest and treat those as your preparation checklist. The questions you cannot answer yet are exactly the ones that will determine whether this works.

**Fewer than 14: You are not ready for this conversation yet.** That is worth knowing now rather than after you have signed a franchise agreement and committed several hundred thousand dollars. Go back through each section and make a plan for what it would take to get to yes. When you can answer most of these honestly, come back.

---

**A note on the scoring:** The number is directional, not a pass/fail. Some questions carry more weight than others. Financial readiness and franchisor due diligence misses are more consequential than others. If you want to talk through where you landed and what it means for your specific situation, that is a useful first conversation.
