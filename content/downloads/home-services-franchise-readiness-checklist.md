# Home Services Franchise Readiness Checklist

**Kelsey Stuart | Waypoint Franchise Advisors | waypointfranchise.com**

---

This is the checklist I use before any first conversation with someone seriously looking at a home services franchise. It covers the specific demands of the category: crew management, local market development, operational discipline, and why the low entry cost does not mean low complexity.

Go through each question honestly.

---

## Section 1: Financial Readiness

- [ ] Do you have liquid capital available for the total investment plus at least 20% in reserve for ramp-up costs beyond the FDD estimate?
- [ ] Have you budgeted reserves to cover personal living expenses for at least 12 months while the business is building its client base?
- [ ] Do you understand that most home services franchises generate minimal revenue in months one through three as local market development takes hold?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 7 of the FDD and mapped every cost line, including vehicle, equipment, initial marketing, and working capital?
- [ ] If financing is involved, have you confirmed with a franchise-specialist lender that you qualify?

---

## Section 2: Crew Management Temperament

- [ ] Are you comfortable hiring, training, scheduling, and managing field crews or technicians?
- [ ] Have you managed teams of hourly or trade workers before, or do you have a clear plan to develop that skill before launch?
- [ ] Do you understand that crew reliability and retention will be your primary operational challenge in year one?
- [ ] Are you prepared to personally cover field work during gaps in crew coverage, at least in the early months?
- [ ] Can you maintain consistent quality standards across multiple crew members without being present on every job?

---

## Section 3: Local Market Development

- [ ] Are you comfortable making local sales calls, canvassing commercial accounts, or networking to build your initial client base?
- [ ] Do you understand that home services franchises do not come with a built-in customer base and that local market development is your responsibility?
- [ ] Are you prepared to invest in local marketing beyond what the franchisor provides at the national level?
- [ ] Do you have or can you build relationships with property managers, commercial accounts, or HOA contacts who represent recurring revenue?
- [ ] Are you patient enough to spend six to twelve months building recurring client relationships before expecting stable revenue?

---

## Section 4: Operational and Lifestyle Fit

- [ ] Are you comfortable with a business that may require early morning starts, weekend work, or non-standard hours depending on the service model?
- [ ] Do you understand that most home services franchises are not semi-absentee in year one, regardless of how they are marketed?
- [ ] Are you drawn to the operational side of running a crew-based business, not just the idea of owning one?
- [ ] Have you thought through what this business looks like in year three once the market is developed and the team is stable?
- [ ] Have you had an honest conversation with your family or partner about the time commitment during the launch period?

---

## Section 5: Site Selection and Territory

- [ ] Have you identified the territory you want to operate in and confirmed it is available?
- [ ] Do you understand the density and demographics required for the specific home services model you are evaluating?
- [ ] Have you looked at the competitive landscape in your target market, specifically local operators who hold the accounts you would be taking on?
- [ ] Do you understand that your territory defines your total addressable market and that expansion typically requires purchasing additional territory?

---

## Section 6: Franchisor Due Diligence

- [ ] Have you read the full franchise disclosure document for this brand, not just the summary provided by the franchisor?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 19 and calculated realistic revenue based on actual franchisee-reported performance, not projected figures?
- [ ] Have you spoken directly with at least five existing franchisees from the FDD list (not referrals selected by the franchisor)?
- [ ] Have you asked those franchisees what the first twelve months of market development actually looked like?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 20 to understand franchisee turnover and closure rates in the last three years?

---

## Scoring Guide

Count how many boxes you checked.

**22 to 24: Strong position.** You have done real preparation. A first conversation makes sense now. We can focus on specific brands and territory fit rather than foundational questions.

**16 to 21: Mostly ready, with some gaps.** Review which sections had the most unchecked boxes. Gaps in financial readiness or franchisor due diligence are fixable before moving forward. Gaps in crew management temperament or local market development are worth sitting with longer.

**10 to 15: More work to do.** That is worth knowing now. Use the unchecked items as your preparation list before committing to a process.

**Fewer than 10: Not ready yet.** The gaps are significant enough that starting this process now increases the risk of a poor outcome. Address the foundational items first.
