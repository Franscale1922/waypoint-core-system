# B2B Franchise Readiness Checklist

**Kelsey Stuart | Waypoint Franchise Advisors | waypointfranchise.com**

---

This is the checklist I use before any first conversation with someone seriously looking at a B2B franchise. The category has structural advantages most buyers overlook: recurring contract revenue, commercial clients who make rational purchasing decisions, and no dependence on consumer foot traffic. These questions are designed to help you assess whether you are positioned to take advantage of those strengths.

---

## Section 1: Financial Readiness

- [ ] Do you have liquid capital for the total investment plus working capital reserves for the client acquisition ramp-up period, typically six to twelve months in B2B?
- [ ] Do you understand that B2B revenue builds on contracted relationships and that the first ninety days will likely generate little to no revenue while accounts are being established?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 7 (the section of the franchise disclosure document that details the total estimated investment required to open) and mapped every cost line, including vehicle or equipment requirements, software, uniforms, and territory fees?
- [ ] Have you looked at Item 19 (the section where franchisors may voluntarily disclose financial performance data for existing units) and studied revenue at different tenure levels, specifically comparing year one units to year three and beyond?
- [ ] If financing, have you confirmed qualification with a lender experienced in B2B franchise funding?

---

## Section 2: B2B Sales Capability

- [ ] Are you comfortable making outbound sales calls to business owners, property managers, or commercial accounts?
- [ ] Do you have prior experience in B2B sales, account management, or business development, or a clear plan to develop those skills before launch?
- [ ] Do you understand that the primary driver of early revenue in B2B franchising is your ability to build commercial relationships in person, not marketing or brand recognition?
- [ ] Are you comfortable with a longer sales cycle where deals take weeks or months to close, as opposed to a retail or consumer transaction?
- [ ] Are you prepared to build a referral and relationship network in your local market as a core part of your business strategy?

---

## Section 3: Account Retention Strategy

- [ ] Do you understand that B2B franchise value comes from recurring contracts, not one-time transactions, and that client retention is more important than client acquisition after year one?
- [ ] Have you thought through how you will deliver consistent quality to commercial accounts over time as your client base grows?
- [ ] Are you comfortable managing the relationship side of commercial accounts, including periodic check-ins, handling complaints, and contract renewals?
- [ ] Have you looked at existing franchisee data to understand typical account retention rates and average contract length for this specific brand?

---

## Section 4: Operational Fit

- [ ] Are you comfortable managing field staff or technicians who deliver the service to commercial clients?
- [ ] Do you understand the operational demands of maintaining consistent service quality across multiple accounts simultaneously?
- [ ] Are you prepared to be operationally involved in the business during the launch period, even if the ultimate model is owner-managed with a team?
- [ ] Does the work environment of this specific concept fit your lifestyle? B2B service businesses often involve non-standard hours, commercial settings, or physical work depending on the category.

---

## Section 5: Territory and Market Analysis

- [ ] Have you confirmed that your target territory has sufficient commercial density and the right types of businesses to meet the franchisor's typical revenue projections?
- [ ] Have you looked at who the existing competitors are in your market for this category of service?
- [ ] Do you understand the territory protection terms in the FDD and whether your exclusivity is meaningful given the commercial density of your market?
- [ ] Have you mapped the types of accounts the franchisor's system targets and confirmed those account types exist in meaningful numbers in your territory?

---

## Section 6: Franchisor Due Diligence

- [ ] Have you read the full franchise disclosure document, not the summary provided by the franchisor?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 20 (the section that shows how many franchisees have left the system, and how recently) to understand how many units have opened, closed, or been transferred in the last three years?
- [ ] Have you spoken with at least five existing franchisees from the franchise disclosure document's franchisee list and asked specifically how long it took to reach the revenue level needed to cover all expenses?
- [ ] Have you asked those franchisees what their largest commercial account looks like and how they acquired it?
- [ ] Have you spoken with a franchise attorney who reviewed this specific franchise disclosure document before signing anything?

---

## Scoring Guide

Count how many boxes you checked.

**22 to 24: Strong position.** You understand the B2B model and the work it takes to build it. A first conversation is worth your time. Let's focus on specific brands and territory fit.

**16 to 21: Mostly ready, with some gaps.** B2B sales capability gaps are worth addressing before moving forward, since the early stage of this model is almost entirely driven by your ability to open commercial relationships. Review the unchecked items carefully.

**10 to 15: More to prepare.** The B2B model rewards buyers who understand they are building a sales-driven book of business before anything else. Use the unchecked items as your preparation list.

**Fewer than 10: Not ready yet.** Come back when more of these are yes. The checklist tells you specifically what to work on.
