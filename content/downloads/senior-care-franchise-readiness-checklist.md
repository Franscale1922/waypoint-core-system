# Senior Care Franchise Readiness Checklist

**Kelsey Stuart | Waypoint Franchise Advisors | waypointfranchise.com**

---

This is the checklist I use before any first conversation with someone seriously looking at a senior care franchise. The category has real demographic tailwinds and a recurring revenue structure that is genuinely compelling. It also comes with compliance requirements, caregiver staffing complexity, and a client relationship dynamic that is unlike any other category in franchising. These questions are designed to help you assess whether you are built for it.

---

## Section 1: Financial Readiness

- [ ] Do you have liquid capital for the total investment plus working capital reserves for six to twelve months of operations while your client base is being built?
- [ ] Do you understand that senior care revenue scales gradually as each client relationship is established, and that early-stage cash flow is typically well below mature-unit levels?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 7 (the section of the franchise disclosure document that details the total estimated investment required to open) and mapped every cost line, including licensing, insurance, bonding, uniforms, and territory fees?
- [ ] Have you looked at Item 19 (the section where franchisors may voluntarily disclose financial performance data for existing units) and calculated realistic revenue based on reported caregiver hours per week at different stages of unit maturity?
- [ ] Have you inventoried all available capital sources before considering any loan: liquid savings, accessible investment accounts, home equity (HELOC), and retirement account balances (which may be usable via a ROBS structure)?
- [ ] If you are using a borrowing vehicle to fund any portion of the investment (HELOC, ROBS, or SBA loan as a last resort), have you confirmed you qualify with a lender who has funded senior care franchises before?

---

## Section 2: Regulatory and Licensing Awareness

- [ ] Do you understand that senior care franchises are subject to state-level licensing requirements that vary significantly and that your target state's rules will affect your timeline and legal obligations?
- [ ] Have you confirmed what licensing your target state requires before you can begin serving clients, and how long that process typically takes?
- [ ] Do you understand the insurance and bonding requirements in your state for home care operators?
- [ ] Have you reviewed the franchisor's compliance support resources and confirmed they have experience operating in your specific state?

---

## Section 3: Caregiver Staffing

- [ ] Are you prepared to hire, train, schedule, and retain caregivers, who represent the highest turnover staff category in the healthcare-adjacent services sector?
- [ ] Do you understand that client care quality is entirely dependent on caregiver quality and reliability, and that staffing gaps directly affect client outcomes and renewals?
- [ ] Have you thought through what your recruitment and retention strategy looks like in your specific labor market?
- [ ] Are you comfortable managing a team where the work involves direct care for vulnerable people, and where mistakes have real consequences beyond a typical service business?
- [ ] Have you looked at caregiver wages in your target market and confirmed the margin structure works at those pay levels?

---

## Section 4: Client Relationship and Sales

- [ ] Are you comfortable making sales calls to hospital discharge planners, assisted living facilities, and physician offices to build referral pipelines?
- [ ] Do you understand that B2B referral relationship building is typically how home care clients are acquired, not consumer advertising?
- [ ] Are you comfortable navigating conversations with family members who are making care decisions for aging parents, often in emotionally difficult circumstances?
- [ ] Do you understand that losing a client typically involves a health decline or passing, and that the emotional component of this business is real and ongoing?
- [ ] Are you genuinely motivated by the mission of care, beyond simply evaluating this as a financial investment?

---

## Section 5: Operational Fit

- [ ] Are you prepared to be actively involved in the business, including on-call situations, for at least the first two years?
- [ ] Have you thought through a plan for managing after-hours caregiver call-outs, which are a routine part of running a home care operation?
- [ ] Do you understand that most senior care franchises require the owner to serve as or hire a qualified administrator or director of operations depending on state requirements?
- [ ] Is your lifestyle and family situation compatible with the on-call nature of this business, particularly during the startup phase?

---

## Section 6: Franchisor Due Diligence

- [ ] Have you read the full franchise disclosure document, not just the summary provided by the franchisor?
- [ ] Have you reviewed Item 20 (the section that shows how many franchisees have left the system, and how recently) to understand franchisee turnover and closure rates in the last three years?
- [ ] Have you spoken directly with at least five current franchisees from the franchise disclosure document's franchisee list and asked how long it took to reach financially stable operations?
- [ ] Have you asked those franchisees what their caregiver retention looks like and what they wish they had known before signing?
- [ ] Have you spoken with a franchise attorney who reviewed this specific franchise disclosure document before signing anything?

---

## Scoring Guide

Count how many boxes you checked.

**22 to 24: Strong position.** You understand the demands of this category and are ready to have a serious conversation about which concepts to evaluate. Let's talk.

**16 to 21: Mostly ready, with gaps.** Regulatory and staffing sections are particularly important here. If those have unchecked boxes, spend time on them before moving forward.

**10 to 15: More to prepare.** Senior care rewards buyers who go in with clear eyes on staffing and licensing complexity. Address the gaps before signing anything.

**Fewer than 10: Not ready yet.** Use the checklist as your preparation list. This is a category where the buyer who did their homework performs dramatically better than the one who did not.
