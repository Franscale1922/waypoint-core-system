# The Franchise Dispatch — Issue #1

---

Before most people ever talk to a franchisor, they've spent months in their own head about it. That's normal. And there's something about that first real conversation they usually don't see coming: it's not one-sided.

I spent 12 years on the other side of that table. I awarded franchises to 85+ people and turned down some who had every dollar required. When I was sitting across from a candidate, I wasn't running a background check...I was watching for things that don't show up on a financial statement.

Three of them mattered more than anything else:


How they handled not knowing. The evaluation process keeps candidates in uncertainty on purpose, at least early on. The people who could sit with "I don't have all the information yet" and still ask good questions almost always outperformed the ones who projected confidence they didn't actually have.


How they talked about hard times. Not whether they'd had them. Everyone has. Whether they treated a setback as data they could learn from, or as a verdict about who they were. That one showed up fast.


Whether they asked for help before they needed it. Owners who called in month two consistently did better than the ones who went quiet until month eight. It sounds counterintuitive but it wasn't. The people who asked early were the ones who understood they'd bought into a system, and actually used it.

None of those are things you either have or don't. They're things you can walk into the process knowing about. And that's kind of the whole point of what I'm doing here. The path into franchising is a lot less mysterious than it looks from the outside...once somebody who's been on the inside tells you what they actually saw.

If you've been quietly turning this over, reply and tell me where your head is.

---

Worth sitting with:

Most people spend the early part of this process asking "is this a good franchise?" But that question has the same problem as asking "is this a good house?" It depends entirely on who's moving in. A two-bedroom, one-bath is not a good house for a family of six. A mansion is not a good house for two people who want simplicity. The franchise that changed someone else's life may not be right for yours. The better question is "am I the right person for this model?" That one takes a little more honesty to answer.
