# Waypoint Franchise Archetype Framework
**Reference document for content creation, email copy, quiz logic, and advisor conversations.**

---

## The 8 Archetypes

This framework identifies franchise buyers by their **natural behavioral style** — how they work, not just why they want to own a business. Research basis: FranNet personality matching, DISC profiles, Perplexity franchise industry analysis.

---

### 📚 The Educator
**DISC:** S/I | **MBTI:** ENFJ/ESFJ | **Enneagram:** 2/9

**Core motivation:** To develop others through instruction and knowledge transfer.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Naturally breaks complex topics into teachable steps
- Enjoys creating training materials, SOPs, onboarding processes
- Patient with questions — thrives explaining the "why"
- Gets energy from "aha!" moments
- Prefers structured learning environments over chaos

**Professional background:** Corporate trainer, teacher, instructional designer, L&D, curriculum developer

**Franchise fit:** Thrives where educating customers or staff IS the value proposition.

**Strong fits:** Children's Education & Enrichment, Senior Care, Business Services (Coaching/Consulting), Home Services
**Weak fits:** Food & Beverage (QSR), Junk Removal, Pest Control

**Content angle:** "How your teaching instinct becomes your competitive moat in education franchises" / "Why franchises with strong training systems outperform independent businesses"

---

### 🤝 The Caregiver
**DISC:** S | **MBTI:** INFP/ISFJ | **Enneagram:** 2

**Core motivation:** To serve vulnerable populations through compassionate connection.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Reads emotional undercurrents in rooms and conversations
- Remembers personal details about the people they serve
- Takes rejection personally — needs purpose beyond profit
- Prioritizes harmony, avoids conflict when possible
- Builds deep loyalty through genuine care

**Professional background:** Healthcare, social work, counseling, senior care, childcare, nonprofit

**Franchise fit:** Businesses where trust = client retention = recurring revenue.

**Strong fits:** Senior Care, Pet Services, Children's Education & Enrichment, Medical Wellness
**Weak fits:** Junk Removal, Business Services (B2B Sales), Restoration & Cleaning

**Content angle:** "If you spent your career caring for people, franchise ownership might be the next chapter" / "Why senior care franchises recruit for heart first, skill second"

---

### 🌐 The Connector
**DISC:** I | **MBTI:** ENFP/ENTP | **Enneagram:** 3/7

**Core motivation:** To build and maintain relationships that create opportunities.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Remembers names, faces, personal details effortlessly
- Naturally works rooms — loves "who do you know?" conversations
- Referral generation feels effortless, not forced
- Tracks relationships systematically (natural CRM user)
- Thrives at networking events, chamber functions, community banking

**Professional background:** Business development, account management, partnership roles, referral-based sales

**Franchise fit:** Any referral-heavy business where network size = pipeline size.

**Strong fits:** Senior Care, Beauty & Personal Care, Business Services (Consulting/Coaching), Real Estate & Home Improvement, Medical Wellness
**Weak fits:** Lawn & Landscape, Pest Control, Food & Beverage (QSR)

**Content angle:** "Your LinkedIn network is a franchise asset you haven't monetized yet" / "Which franchise categories reward relationship builders the most"

---

### ⚙️ The Operator
**DISC:** C/S | **MBTI:** ISTJ/ESTJ | **Enneagram:** 1/6

**Core motivation:** To create efficient processes that scale predictably.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Sees inefficiencies everywhere — can't stop fixing them
- Loves SOPs, checklists, training manuals, dispatch software
- Gets frustrated by "that's how we've always done it"
- Tracks metrics naturally (labor %, food cost %, utilization)
- Prefers managing systems over managing people

**Professional background:** Operations management, general management, P&L responsibility, multi-unit retail, distribution

**Franchise fit:** Any category where systems and cost discipline = profit margin.

**Strong fits:** Home Services, Restoration & Cleaning, Fitness & Wellness, Food & Beverage (QSR), Lawn & Landscape, Pest Control
**Weak fits:** Real Estate & Home Improvement (Brokerage), Entertainment & Events

**Content angle:** "Why franchise systems were literally designed for the Operator personality" / "The correlation between process discipline and franchise profitability"

---

### 🎯 The Driver
**DISC:** D | **MBTI:** ENTJ/ESTP | **Enneagram:** 3/8

**Core motivation:** To achieve results through direct action and persuasion.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Comfortable with rejection — treats "no" as next step
- Thrives on quotas, commissions, measurable outcomes
- Direct communication style, "tells it like it is"
- Gets impatient with relationship-building small talk
- Pipeline velocity, close rates, revenue targets = oxygen

**Professional background:** B2B sales, enterprise sales, cold outreach, business development, high-ticket closing

**Franchise fit:** Businesses where daily prospecting IS the business in Year 1.

**Strong fits:** Junk Removal, Real Estate & Home Improvement, Business Services (B2B Sales), Pest Control, Home Services
**Weak fits:** Senior Care, Children's Education & Enrichment, Pet Services

**Content angle:** "Commercial cleaning owners prospect 60-80 hours/week in Year 1 — is that your element?" / "Franchise categories where your close rate is your competitive advantage"

---

### 🏘️ The Community Builder
**DISC:** S/I | **MBTI:** ENFJ | **Enneagram:** 9/2

**Core motivation:** To create belonging and make local impact.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Natural organizer of events, fundraisers, volunteer activities
- Knows everyone in town (chamber, rotary, school boards)
- Creates "movements" around causes
- Energy comes from group momentum and shared mission
- Marketing feels authentic when community-focused

**Professional background:** Community banking, nonprofit leadership, chamber of commerce, local government, events

**Franchise fit:** Local-market businesses where owner personality becomes the brand.

**Strong fits:** Fitness & Wellness, Children's Education & Enrichment, Pet Services, Beauty & Personal Care, Entertainment & Events
**Weak fits:** Business Services (B2B Sales), Pest Control, Junk Removal

**Content angle:** "How boutique fitness studios turn community energy into a 3-year referral flywheel" / "The franchise owner who already knows half their customer base before they open"

---

### 📊 The Analyst
**DISC:** C | **MBTI:** INTJ/ISTJ | **Enneagram:** 5/1

**Core motivation:** To optimize through data, systems, and financial discipline.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Asks "what's the data?" before making decisions
- Builds Excel models for everything (job costing, LTV, CAC)
- Finds patterns others miss, thinks in systems
- Risk-averse — needs to see numbers work before committing
- Gets frustrated by "vibes-based" decision making

**Professional background:** Financial analysis, P&L management, operations analysis, strategy, accounting

**Franchise fit:** Cost-control intensive businesses where rigor = margin protection.

**Strong fits:** Restoration & Cleaning, Medical Wellness, Food & Beverage (QSR), Lawn & Landscape, Pest Control, Pool & Outdoor
**Weak fits:** Entertainment & Events, Real Estate & Home Improvement (Brokerage), Senior Care

**Content angle:** "The franchise buyer who reads Item 19 before the sales deck" / "Why restaurant franchise failure rates drop when the owner is an Analyst"

---

### 🎨 The Creative
**DISC:** I | **MBTI:** INFP/ENFP | **Enneagram:** 4/7

**Core motivation:** To differentiate through aesthetics, messaging, experience.

**Behavioral tells:**
- Visual thinker — notices design details everywhere
- Creates Instagram-worthy experiences instinctively
- Thinks in stories, metaphors, emotional connection
- Trend-aware, early adopter of platforms and tools
- Gets bored with "good enough," always refining presentation

**Professional background:** Marketing, branding, creative direction, social media, design, media

**Franchise fit:** Businesses where brand experience at the local level IS the differentiator.

**Strong fits:** Beauty & Personal Care, Fitness & Wellness (Boutique), Entertainment & Events, Children's Education & Enrichment
**Weak fits:** Lawn & Landscape, Pest Control, Junk Removal, Restoration & Cleaning

**Content angle:** "Your Instagram instinct is a franchise marketing superpower — in the right category" / "Why beauty franchise success ties directly to local social engagement"

### 1. Senior Care
**Brands:** Visiting Angels, Right at Home, Senior Helpers, SYNERGY HomeCare, Amada Senior Care, HomeWell Care Services, Assisting Hands, CarePatrol, Griswold, A Place At Home, Home Helpers, Comfort Keepers

**Business model:** Non-medical in-home care billed at $24-$32/hour. B2C dominant with private-pay clients, LTC insurance, VA benefits, Medicaid waivers. 70-80% recurring weekly revenue. Net margins 10-20%.

**Investment range:** $52K-$430K (most cluster $90K-$200K)

**Year 1 owner role (55-60 hrs/wk):**
- 35% Caregiver recruiting, hiring, retention
- 25% Sales and referral network (hospitals, discharge planners, senior communities, elder law attorneys)
- 25% Client intake, care plan coordination, family communication
- 15% Admin, scheduling, billing, compliance

**Top archetype fits:** Caregiver ★★★★★ | Connector ★★★★☆ | Community Builder ★★★★☆
**Poor fits:** Driver, Analyst

**Key stat:** 61.2 million Americans age 65+ (2024), projected 80M+ by 2040

## Quiz Structure

**8 questions. Multi-dimensional scoring. Each answer contributes points to multiple archetypes simultaneously.**

### Question Logic
Each answer carries a `scores` map — e.g. `{ educator: 4, community_builder: 1 }`. After all 8 answers, scores are summed and the highest total wins.

This means:
- No single question determines your archetype
- Consistent answers produce a sharply typed result
- Mixed answers produce a middle-ground archetype
- The scoring surface is wide enough to differentiate all 8 types

### The 8 Questions

| # | Dimension | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Energy source | What work leaves you energized at day's end |
| 2 | Natural strength | What people consistently come to you for |
| 3 | Discomfort | What work you quietly dread |
| 4 | People style | How you naturally operate with others |
| 5 | Rejection response | What happens when a prospect says no or a plan fails |
| 6 | Business metrics | What number you'd wake up thinking about |
| 7 | Career background | Which professional chapter best fits |
| 8 | Ownership vision | What success looks like 3 years in |

## Content Creation Uses

**Blog post angles:**
- "Which franchise archetype are you?" (quiz CTA)
- "Why [Archetype] buyers succeed in [Industry]" — 8 x 16 = 128 potential pieces
- "The biggest mistake [Archetype] buyers make when choosing a franchise"
- "Day 1-365 of franchise ownership: what it actually looks like for [Archetype]"

**Email nurture angles:**
- Post-quiz confirmation email: speak directly to the archetype's motivations and fears
- Follow-up sequences: industry matches surfaced by archetype
- Re-engagement: "You took the quiz 30 days ago — here's what [Archetype] buyers are asking me right now"

**Social media angles:**
- Short-form video: "You're a [Archetype] if..." with behavioral tells
- Carousels: Archetype x Industry matrix
- Story polls: "Which of these sounds most like you?" → quiz CTA

**Discovery call prep:**
- Use quiz result to pre-qualify conversation angle before the call
- Archetype shapes which industry categories to surface first
- Advisor note language modeled how to speak to each type
